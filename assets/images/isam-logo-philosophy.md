# Design Philosophy — "Scholarly Gravity"

A visual language for a serious, peer-reviewed journal. The work embodies the
quiet authority of institutions that have measured, classified, and advanced
human knowledge for centuries. It rejects ornament, gradient, and novelty —
the only currency here is **precision**.

**Form and space.** The square is not background; it is the field of
attention. A single, generous inset frame establishes a margin of silence
around the monogram, the way a museum plinth elevates an artifact. All
elements sit on strict optical center; no decorative offsets, no diagonals,
no tilted axes. The geometry is as disciplined as a scientific instrument.

**Color.** One pigment: a deep, almost navy blue — `#0b2c5a` — the color of
university seals, clinical linen, old maps. Its single accent is pure white,
used sparingly as light carved out of the blue. No shadows, no transparency
gradients. The flatness is intentional; it signals that every pixel was
placed by a maker, not rendered by a machine.

**Typography.** A geometric sans of uncompromising weight, set large enough
to feel monumental but with letter-forms kept calm — no stylistic flourishes,
no italics, no compressed widths. The monogram "iSM" reads as three characters
of equal visual presence, a shorthand for the journal's title. Beneath it,
the full name is tracked out wide and small, the way a cornerstone inscribes
an institution's purpose — present but in service of the mark above.

**Craftsmanship.** Every detail evidences long consideration: the stroke weight
balanced against the frame, the optical spacing between letters corrected by
hand, the baseline rhythm of the sub-text refined until it reads as a single
breath. This is the visual equivalent of a well-edited manuscript — nothing
superfluous, everything exact.

**Scale and rhythm.** The logo must speak at 1024px and whisper at 200px.
Strokes are thickened deliberately so that at thumbnail size the mark
remains crisp, unambiguous, and recognizable. The hierarchy is rigid:
monogram dominates, frame steadies, subtitle grounds.

**The overall effect.** Not a decoration, but a seal. When a reader sees it
beside a DOI, they should sense the weight of a venue that has done the work
of peer review, archival preservation, and long-form scholarly stewardship.
