## Introduction

A human digital disease model integrates multi-scale biological, clinical, and
environmental data into a computable representation of disease. Such models aim
to simulate progression, stratify patients, and test interventions *in silico*
before clinical deployment.

## Scope

- **Multi-omics integration**: genome, transcriptome, proteome, metabolome
  layered on patient-level phenotypes.
- **Mechanistic + data-driven hybrids**: pairing physiology-based models with
  machine learning components.
- **Patient-specific calibration**: fitting model parameters from EHR, imaging,
  and wearable streams.
- **Validation**: holdout cohorts, prospective trials, and registry-linked
  outcomes.

## Applications

- Treatment response prediction and optimization
- Clinical trial *in silico* cohorts
- Disease progression forecasting
- Counterfactual reasoning for intervention design
